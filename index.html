About Me

I answer tough questions in complex product spaces: AR/VR hardware and wearables, developer tools, advertising, and aviation. At Meta, I lead hardware UX research for a new VR device. At Google, I ran large-scale surveys and focus groups to shape product strategy for Flutter. My background in mechanical engineering helps me work closely with hardware and software teams, ask the right questions, and get to the root of a problem. I use a mix of qualitative and quantitative methods and communicate findings in ways that are clear and easy to act on.

When I’m not doing UX research, I channel my curiosity into exploring the world, optimizing my garden, or perfecting my macaronage. If you share my interests, whether personal or professional, feel free to say hello!
